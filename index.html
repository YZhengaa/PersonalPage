DiCon ontology suite

The DiCon (Digital Construction Ontology) is a modular ontology suitedeveloped to formally represent and integrate construction workflow information in the context of digital construction. It addresses the challenge of information heterogeneity by providing a shared vocabulary and structure that enables the integration of data from diverse systems and contexts. DiCon consists of six core modules—Entities, Processes, Information, Agents, Variables, and Contexts—designed to capture the complexity of construction workflows information.

DiCon-SII ontology

The DiCon Semantic Image Interpretation (DiCon-SII) is a ontology developed to enhance the interpretation and integration of construction images within digital construction systems. It addresses two key challenges: the semantic gap between low-level image features and high-level construction concepts, and the lack of integration between images and systems like BIM and construction schedules. DiCon-SII provides a structured vocabulary for visual construction content and acts as a bridge to enable image-supported Digital Twins for Construction (DTC).

DiCon-CPL

The DiCon Construction Process Library (DiCon CPL) ontology introduces a semantic framework for modeling construction state inference rules using Semantic Web technologies. It combines ontology-based metadata with SHACL-based rule definitions to promote rule shareability and reuse. By utilizing the DiCon ontology as a consistent terminology base, the rules can be applied across different construction data sources. These rules are organized into a shared library, enabling users to search, retrieve, and reuse them efficiently.

DiCon Semantic Digital Twin

The DiCon-SDT is a framework designed to enable effective monitoring of dynamic construction workflows through semantic Digital Twins. It addresses challenges in data integration and modeling by leveraging an ontology network that formalizes diverse construction data types and feeds them into a semantic DT data lake. This setup supports functions of simulation and data interpretation, and thus achieve progress monitoring and predictive analysis.

DiCon-RCA (Ongoing)

The DiCon-RCA (Robotic Context Awareness) is a ontology-based framework that facilitates adaptive robotic navigation in dynamic construction environments. It semantically integrates digital construction data, robotic information, and environmental context using semantic web ontologies. This enables industrial robots to interpret real-world site conditions, understand their surroundings, and autonomously navigate and execute tasks on construction sites.br>

DiCon-SE ontology

The DiCon Smart Elevator (DiCon-SE) ontology is an extension of the DiCon ontology, specifically developed to model smart elevator logistics data in construction. It captures detailed elevator movement events, including where and when elevators moved, and what materials or loads they carried or offloaded—supporting logistics and operational management on construction sites. Built using data from the Kone smart elevator API, DiCon-SE combines static metadata (e.g., elevator properties and building-sensor relationships) with dynamic tracking data.

OCQA ontology

The Ontology for Construction Quality Assurance (OCQA) is designed to formally represent and standardize knowledge relevant to quality inspection planning in construction. OCQA supports flexible and scalable customization via trade-specific extensions, allowing users to adapt the ontology according to project-specific terminologies, regulations, and requirements. Its modular and extensible design ensures applicability across diverse construction scenarios and simplifies future ontology expansions.

InPro ontology

The Industrial Production Workflow Ontology (InPro) is designed to formalize and integrate data within industrial production processes to address interoperability challenges in Industry 4.0. Built around the 5M model—manpower, machines, materials, methods, and measurements—the ontology consists of seven core modules: Entities, Agents, Machines, Materials, Methods, Measurements, and Production Processes. The Machines module specifically leverages the OPC Unified Architecture (OPC UA) information model.
